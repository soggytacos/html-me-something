Biography for Launchcode

HTML Me Something

I received my Bachelor’s degree in Economics from Truman State University in 2015. I know, I’m a whipper-snapper! While in school, I worked for Monsanto’s Seed Technology Program as a Compliance Coordinator assessing farms for compliance of the EPA’s seed technology guidelines. After graduating, I spent a year and a half working at Tesla Motors St. Louis, waiting tables at night, and working as a handyman. In November 2016, ACI snatched me up to become the Dispatch Software Specialist in St. Louis. Currently, my main role in the company is to oversee the implementation of our new scanning system, Next Stop Mobile.

The most important thing I learned from my previous profession is the importance of being adaptive in the face of change. A hundred years ago, an industrious farmer was one that learned how to employ his livestock and multi-animal equipment effectively in daily farm operations. Today, GPS-automated tractors, advanced chemical formulation, and genetically modified seeds have made farming vastly more efficient. The result is that the average farm today is 400 acres larger, produces nearly ten times as much per acre, requires 50% less water per bushel, and requires nearly 90% fewer man hours per bushel. In the year 1900, 40% of Americans were employed by agriculture.

Today, the number is less than 2%! The intent of this brief history lesson is to get readers thinking about why this paragraph matters in the context of ACI as a player in the logistics industry. The future is never certain, and the present is always changing. We must understand that the path to success requires us to think strategically, to fear nothing, and to be adaptable in the face of change.
